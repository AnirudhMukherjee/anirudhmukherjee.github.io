About 2020.

Anirudh Mukherjee
Jan 1, 2021 6 min read Thoughts

Man, what a year. I could go ahead and give you an analogy that paints 2020 alongside some situation that goes absolutely bonkers, but I shall refrain from doing so. We all know how it went. The whole year (or at least most of it) were some of the most trying times we might have faced in recent memory. I don’t think I have personally witnessed any such event in my lifetime given that I’m still considerably young (but not for long, haha…) which had such a massive impact worldwide. And as I am writing this, the virus still rages on. Although this time, there seems to be a glimmer of light shining at the end of this dark tunnel. That’s the phase we are in now, I call this the hope phase. Throughout our journey through this tunnel we’ve all had our own personal struggles, some caused by the ongoing pandemic and some catalyzed by it. If you’ve managed to see the light of this day, congratulations. You’ve come out stronger. And as we all have been saying, you survived 2020.

Before I go on rambling about this year, I must say that every single word jotted down over here comes from the point of view of a very privileged individual. The year was way worse.

If I had one word to describe this year, it would be uncertainty. As the world locked itself inside, a large number of questions started popping up in everyone’s minds. Questions that did not have a clear answer back then. These were uncharted territories we were venturing out into, something we were never trained for. Something that was as easy as staying indoors but something that was as challenging as, well, staying indoors. We went inside without a clear idea of when it would be possible to come out again. And that’s when anxiety slapped us hard. We had no clear idea about our future, everyone’s plans were suddenly hacked and slashed into pieces. We were worried about our livelihoods, layoffs, education, economy, viral tiktoks, the American elections and lastly… the health of ours and our loved ones. We were distanced. The future was virtual. Virtual meets, virtual stand ups, virtual movie time, virtual classrooms, just take whatever you used to do before the pandemic happened and add the word virtual before it and voila, you’re in the future. Future was the key player looming over everyone’s heads while they had to cope with another important player in the game, the present. And boy, coping was hard.

I was in my final year of engineering when the unspeakable happened - traditional day at college. Coincidentally it was also my birthday. Also coincidentally, it was my last day at college before they were closed off. So yeah signing off on a very auspicious day, I was naive enough to think this would be easy back then. I was super happy to just sit at home and wait for this virus thing to pass. Two days later, I wore a mask for the first time in my life as I went to visit my significant other after her exams (which were called off midway). It was indeed very weird as very few people outside donned a mask, not to say that I looked like an absolute joker because I was wearing it upside down. But then in a week or so, a complete lockdown order had been issued for the entire country. Everyone was home. Just, passing time. Just need to pass some more time. Okay we’re almost there. Nope, some more time. And so it went on. There was no end.

I had just been accepted to my top choice of graduate school and this is exactly when my worries began. I could not see a solution to our problem in the near time and I did not want to risk beginning my studies in the middle of a pandemic. More importantly I did not want to leave this way, without saying proper goodbyes to my loved ones. I was worried left, right and center about how things would pan out. Eventually my university let us all defer our admissions by one year and I am so glad and thankful to them for being this considerate towards their prospective students. Even the American elections raised my BP through the roof but I won’t comment on that. Indoor exercises, art, programming, video games, books, making this damn website and mindlessly wandering around on the internet kept me busy most of the time. But every few weeks or so I would break down. And if you did too, that’s okay. It’s completely normal. Getting back up was another recurring thing that I did this year since I was being knocked down quite too often for my own taste. Which is my cue to insert this very motivational quote said by one of my favourite boxers of all time. Okay, jokes aside, it is definitely relevant. Sorry if the picture is too much of what you see on your virtual family groups.

Every so often something or the other would creep up in my mind. Something twisted. The past, the future or the fact that I still hadn’t learned how to make a proper roti. Sitting alone by yourself does that to you. All forms of thoughts come and go. Insidious ones, quite often if you’re like me. All the free time I had got me to speculate all the different possible scenarios for my future, just like Dr. Strange did in that movie. Right down to the point where I started questioning my own sanity. This was not good for my mind, so I started meditating, writing down my thoughts, laughing at how stupid my thoughts were, eliminating stressors through lists and talking besides the million other things I got myself into to keep myself sane. I barely saw any new faces for a couple of months. I missed people. I missed my partner the most. We would video call each other at nights and that would be the happiest moment of my day. So yeah, it was difficult. There were a lot many challenges this year that were completely new to me and got me by surprise.

This was a year of uncertainty. Solidarity in uncertainty. Hope in uncertainty. Acceptance in uncertainty. And perseverance in uncertainty. This was the year I got to learn how to face my biggest fear- uncertainty itself. Not that I am a black belt at it yet, but I did learn a lot. This was a year of reflectance for all of us, for I am sure we must have had those days when we sat down and had a good hard look at ourselves and our very own lives. This was a year of courage. All of us deserve to give ourselves a pat on our backs because no matter how low things got this year, we pushed through. And we made it. If you’re still struggling through some rough tides, hang in there buddy. Things will get better. Remember, one day at a time.

Welcome to 2021. Have an amazing year ahead.
